How are electricity service deficiencies affecting the routine of remote workers in the city of Barranquilla?

Where are we?

Barranquilla, bathed with the Caribbean waters, sits in northern Colombia. Well-known for its carnival, vibrant music, warmth and for being a gate from the country's interior to the rest of the world. Its average temperature is 27 with some peaks during dry season of 32.

Its metropolitan area hosts more than 2 million people that have lived a complicated relationship with their electricity providers for decades.

What happens?

A mix of financial, organizational and political issues have caused people not trusting the electricity service, making blackouts, damaged appliances, elevated costs and even loss of life the bread and butter of not only the city but the whole Caribbean region.

  • Infrastructure is outdated or not sufficient
  • User bills are deliberately altered
  • Planned and unplanned blackouts in defined areas of the region
  • Illegal connections

Growing conlict

Electricity companies have gone bankrupt or its directives convicted and then another comes and the story is repeated. Press and social media have recorded years of disappointment and growing rejection towards one service provider after the other.

The causes...?

There hasnt been one root cause identified yet. What people believe is that the problem is a snowball. Mistrust causes that people ilegally connect to the grid or avoid paying their bills, which causes financial instability to the provider, which causes the infrastructure to run outdated, which causes failures in the service, and so on and so on.

The people

Three remote workers who live in Barranquilla tell us their experiences and show us how their routines are changing. Three different perspectives of the problem and how to manage it.

Janeth

Has lived in multiple areas of the city looking for more comfortable conditions with no success.

Now shes the mother of a girl and that adds one more preoccupation to the situation

Janeth lives in a two-bedroom apartment

She lives with her husband and their 2-year-old daughter

She and her husband work from home, the baby goes to the kindergarten

Spends her free time doing digital illustrations

Electricity in Janeths home

As a remote worker, electricity is also a source of income. Janeth and her husband rely on energy for their laptops and internet connection to work.

They have around 4 devices each, among laptop, desktop computer, tablets, headphones, monitors

They dont own a TV, they normally use their computers for entertainment after work.

Their most valued appliances are fans because heat can be annoying, specially in the afternoon.

They have air conditioning installed in the two bedrooms but they only use it in the babys room, to save costs.

In the kitchen they only have the refrigerator and a few appliances that she calls secondary using electricity. The stove uses gas.

How does Janeth see the problem?

She says there have always been deficiencies in the electricity supply service. She cant even recall when it started but she started noticing more beginning with COVID pandemic.

When spending more time at home, she could tell the difference. Public spaces are normally prepared for outages but residential areas are normally more affected.

She has moved two times because of problems related to energy supply. The first time because of constant unannounced blackouts, the second because of the high costs.

Shes planning on moving again when her current contract ends because the area is particularly hot and the cost of electricity is too elevated.

For her, the constant and prolonged power outages makes her feel repellent to the provider company and the perception is that the price they need to pay for energy is not proportional to what is given.

She often avoids spending electricity just as retaliation for the bad service.

(Price of) water has also increased but we have a good service, it doesnt hurt to pay what its worth

(El agua tambin ha subido pero tenemos buen servicio, no duele pagar lo que cuesta)

The day to day

Outages can be planned or unplanned. By law, providers need to display notices in mass media at least 24 hours before planned outages. Janeth says some times this is not enough for her to be aware of it: you will need to be on Instagram all day to see their posts.

Often, outages are planned to last 6 hours but end up leaving neighborhoods with no electricity for full days.

In terms of blackouts, Janeth turns to her mother. As electricity is not cut in the whole city at the same time, she can go to her moms to spend the day.

These movements are easy now because both her husband and her bought laptops and a car. Also the newborn brought additional obstacles that she later managed to sort out.

Another option is to go and work at a mall. She says that she can go and work from a caf or free co-working spaces that malls are increasingly building. Sometimes you need to be there at 8 AM to get a spot because people have taken malls as their default workspace.

People in Barranquilla have identified that electricity service works differently in every sector. Neighborhoods more to the south, which have also lower income, experience more constant and unannounced outages.

She used to live close to a hospital which, the urban legend says, can assure less outages as the circuit will need to be well maintained.

Preparing for a blackout is tough, it is not like you can collect electricity to use while there is no power

(Prepararse para un apagn es complicado, no es como que puedas recoger electricidad para usarla despus)

How to cope with it

What Janeth will normally do is to try to spend less electricity by cutting usage on some appliances or going elsewhere to work.

She needs to keep her laptop and phone charged, awaiting for a sudden blackout. And if its not enough, she will use the buildings shared areas which are normally powered by generators.

The only immediate solution for Janeths family is to run away form blackouts, look for an alternative location.

Janeth doesnt think theres something else to do in the short term. The couple recently bought some land to build a house and they are planning their budget to have solar panels installed.

She also sees no solution for the whole conflict. It is a vicious cycle between embezzlement, illegal connections and politics.

Her perception is that there are people behind the energy providers who are benefitting from the instability and wont let any solutions to flourish.

This will remain the same for the children of my children

(Seguir igual para los hijos de mis hijos)

Sebastian

Since he moved a few months ago, he noticed that electricity service failures were not affecting him too much.

Now he makes an analysis of what the causes for this problem could be.

Sebastian lives in a two-bedroom apartment

He lives with his brother

He and his brother work from home

He is a tech and gaming fan

Electricity at Sebastian's

Living with his brother leaves him little responsibility beyond taking care of himself. Most of his day is spent working remotely a s a Project Manager, for which he uses the company laptop.

After working, Sebastian likes hacking his personal desktop computer and playing video games. This is a high-tech, delicate appliance which he takes care of a lot.

Air conditioning is on all day long, he says he wouldnt leave without it. He considers Barranquilla is one of the hottest areas in the country and he cannot imagine how people would survive without having the temperature regulated.

He owns multiple appliances that he uses occasionally, such as toaster, air fryer, waffle maker, TVs, etc.

How does Sebastian see the problem?

For him, the situation has been improving with the years, especially with the arrival of a new provider (which is actually the replacement for one that went bankrupt).

Blackouts last shorter and they are less recurrent, But still happen. What worries him now is that voltage is not stable and sudden outages can damage his appliances

I once bought a Samsung microwave... it didnt last two weeks. There was an outage and it was lost, money was lost completely. I have had my TV and air fryer repaired because of that too.

This kind of events add up to Sebastians electricity bill somehow, he needs to plan a budget for it.

He analyses that the high cost of electricity is due to illegal connections, for which the general population is charged. The other reason is because its used as a consumption discouraging tactic to control the overly-used air conditioning.

He has heard of people that hack their electricity counters with a magnet, which makes it not to run and therefore they will be charged less. Also, his impression is that electricity is produced in the region but it is sold to other countries. This is why he states that the problem is not only infrastructural but cultural and political.

In the Colombian Caribbean we have had a very toxic relationship with the companies that provide energy

(En el caribe colombiano hemos tenido una relacin muy txica con las empresas que proveen energa)

The day to day

Sebastian has outage-proofed his home. He has a UPS for his expensive computer, a surge protector for the fridge and a satellite internet modem for not interrupting his workday.

5,4,3,2,1, happy new year! and the light goes out, it was so funny because its part of our lives. Sebastian referred to the frequency of outages in past years with this personal story. He says theyre used to it but that doesnt stop them from feeling disappointed.

He considers he cannot afford to have his laptop or phone uncharged, because in any circumstance, he needs to assure his availability for work.

Sebastians acquaintances have decided to move just because either the costs or (in)continuity of the service is unbearable.

What people would normally do is relying on relatives that live in different neighborhoods to spend the day. Or, alternatively go to malls which will have for sure electricity supply.

Seeing at other peoples experiences, he feels lucky. His perception is that lower income neighborhoods are less cared for and often the ones that are charged more. He sees almost every month a story in the news showing how people explode in the electricity provider offices because they cant control the situation any more.

Unfortunately it hasnt been as fast as we expected [...] there should be a migration [to new energy sources] but in the hands of public administration it is going too slow

(Desafortunadamente no ha sido tan acelerado como esperbamos [...] debera haber una migracin [hacia nuevas fuentes de energa] pero va muy lento en manos de lo pblico)

How to cope with it

Currently, Sebastian lives in an area surrounded by hospitals, to which he attributes the fact that he hasnt had any prolonged outage since he moved in, 6 months ago.

He says, in the past, hospitals have filed lawsuits because of uncertainty in the continuity of electricity supply in such a delicate environment. This has made the providers to take special care of the infrastructure around medical facilities.

Sebastian believes the residential market hasnt found efficient solutions to the problem (which is not their fault, to begin with). On the other hand, commercial and manufacturing spaces have seen the need to implement total emergency substitution systems to ensure continuous supply. Reason why people use commercial spaces, such as malls, to spend the time while outages last.

From his point of view, he can only protect his belongings and patiently wait if and when a blackout occurs. He also observes that people dont look for any different alternatives apart from having solar panels (if you can afford them).

There have been cases of (very) high income communities that are not paying for the electrical supply. His call for the people is to be conscious or the problem will continue. Avoiding payment or illegally connecting to the grid will only make the problem worse.

One can recognize that there has been evolution and improvement, however, we havent got to the ideal state

(Ha habido una evolucin y una mejora, sin embargo, no hemos llegado al estado ideal)

She hopes to be autonomous and have control over her familys consumption.
